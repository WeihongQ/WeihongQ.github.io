Short bio. I am a computational social scientist and fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at University of Rochester (UR). Besides my Ph.D. studies, I am also a concurrent M.S. in Data Science student at UR. Prior to joining UR, I obtained my M.A. in Public Policy Studies from University of Chicago and B.A. in Economics from Wuhan University.

Research. I am broadly interested in machine learning (ML), data mining and natural language processing, with specific focus on social computing and unsupervised machine learning. My recent research involves: (1) uncovering the sources of news media bias and ineffectiveness of scientific communications; (2) public perceptions on artificial intelligence.

  • Keywords: machine learning, data mining, social computing, media bias, data in politics
