Short bio. I am a computational social scientist and fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at University of Rochester (UR). Besides my Ph.D. studies, I am also a concurrent M.S. in Data Science student at UR. Prior to UR, I obtained my M.A. in Public Policy Studies from University of Chicago and B.A. in Economics (with minor in Applied Mathematics) from Wuhan University.

Research. I am broadly interested in machine learning (ML), data mining and natural language processing (NLP), with specific focus on social computing, causal inference and political research methods. My recent research involves: (1) identifying the origins and implications of media bias; (2) examining the causal effects of natural resource endowments and related policies; (3)causal assessments of factors influencing political opinions and behaviors.
